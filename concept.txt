Abstract:
The integration of blockchain technology with quantum-resistant cryptography for healthcare data management has the potential to revolutionize how sensitive 
medical information is stored, shared, and accessed. Blockchain provides a decentralized, immutable ledger for storing healthcare records, ensuring data 
integrity and security. However, with the rise of quantum computing, traditional cryptographic algorithms may become vulnerable. This paper proposes a 
quantum-resistant, blockchain-based decentralized communication system for healthcare, utilizing advanced cryptographic algorithms such as Kyber and Falcon-512, 
along with Zero-Knowledge Proofs (ZKPs) for enhanced privacy. The system enables secure, patient-controlled data sharing, cross-chain interoperability, 
and scalable healthcare communication without relying on IoT infrastructure. By combining quantum-safe encryption with blockchain's 
inherent transparency and immutability, the proposed solution aims to provide a future-proof framework for managing healthcare data in a post-quantum era.

Introduction:
The healthcare sector faces significant challenges related to data security, privacy, and interoperability. Traditional healthcare data management systems, 
although robust, often rely on centralized models that can be prone to data breaches, unauthorized access, and inefficient data sharing. Blockchain technology,
 with its decentralized and immutable ledger, has emerged as a promising solution for addressing these challenges, ensuring secure, transparent, 
and verifiable data exchanges. However, as quantum computing advances, the cryptographic algorithms that underpin current blockchain systems, 
such as RSA and ECDSA, are at risk of being compromised.

This project proposes a quantum-resistant blockchain-based decentralized communication system designed specifically for healthcare. By leveraging quantum-safe 
cryptographic algorithms like Kyber for key exchanges and Falcon-512 for digital signatures, this system ensures that healthcare data remains secure even in
 the face of quantum computing threats. In addition, the system incorporates Zero-Knowledge Proofs (ZKPs) to allow patients to verify data access requests without
 exposing sensitive information, thus maintaining privacy while facilitating secure data sharing.

The aim of this project is to develop a scalable, software-only solution that enables patient-controlled data sharing, promotes cross-chain interoperability 
for seamless data exchange across institutions, and provides a framework for emergency protocols in healthcare settings. By implementing these technologies, 
the system seeks to provide a future-proof solution for decentralized, secure, and efficient healthcare data management in a post-quantum world.
